What Is Weightlifting?

Weightlifting, also known as resistance training or strength training, involves using weights to improve muscle strength and endurance. It can be done with free weights, machines, or bodyweight exercises.
Benefits:

  • Increases muscle strength and size
  • Improves bone density
  • Enhances metabolism and fat loss

Why Weightlifting?

It builds lean muscle, increases metabolism, and improves bone density and joint strength,
also Enhances posture, balance, brain health, and mental well-being

Getting Started

Start with basic exercises like squats, deadlifts, bench presses, and rows, Also begin with light weights to master form and technique.
Focus on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups.
Gradually increase weight and intensity as you become more comfortable.

Starter Routine

A beginner's routine could include:

  • Squats: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Bench Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Bent-over Rows: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8-12 reps

When you are comfortable with the above, you can focus on parts of the body, such as:

  • Chest: Bench Press, Incline Dumbbell Press
  • Back: Pull-ups, Bent-over Rows
  • Legs: Squats, Lunges
  • Shoulders: Overhead Press, Lateral Raises
  • Arms: Bicep Curls, Tricep Dips

What Is Calisthenics?

Calisthenics is a form of exercise that uses body weight for resistance, focusing on movements like push-ups, pull-ups, squats, and planks.
Benefits:

  • Improves strength, flexibility, and endurance
  • Enhances body control and coordination
  • Can be done anywhere with no equipment needed

Why Practice Calisthenics?

It builds functional strength, improves mobility, and enhances overall fitness. It also burns calories efficiently, supports fat loss, and improves cardiovascular health
Also, it is accessible to all fitness levels and can be adapted to individual needs.

Getting Started

Start with basic movements like push-ups, squats, and planks.
Focus on mastering form and technique before progressing to advanced variations.
Gradually increase difficulty by adding reps, sets, or variations like one-arm push-ups or pistol squats.

Sample Routine

A beginner's routine could include:

  • Push-ups: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Bodyweight Squats: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
  • Planks: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds
  • Pull-ups or Assisted Pull-ups: 3 sets of 5-10 reps

As you progress, you can incorporate more advanced movements like muscle-ups, handstand push-ups, and planches.

What Is Bulk/Cut?

Bulk/Cut refers to the two phases of body composition training:
Bulking focuses on gaining muscle mass, while cutting aims to reduce body fat while preserving muscle.
Benefits:

  • Allows for controlled muscle gain and fat loss
  • Helps achieve a defined physique
  • Provides a structured approach to body composition

Why Bulk/Cut?

It allows for strategic muscle gain and fat loss, helping you achieve your desired physique.
Bulking provides the necessary calories and nutrients for muscle growth, while cutting focuses on fat loss without sacrificing muscle.
It is a common approach among bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts.

How to Bulk/Cut?

To bulk, consume a calorie surplus with a focus on protein and strength training. During a bulk, Moderate surplus (~300-500 kcal/day), protein ~1.3-1.8 g per kg body weight, strength workouts 3-5x/week
To cut, create a calorie deficit while maintaining high protein intake and continuing strength training. During a cut, it is best to do light cardio, gradually decrease calories and monitor progress
Monitor progress and adjust nutrition and training as needed.
